Meet our stars that make us feeling proud:

Goalkeepers

#1. Manuel Neuer

Manuel Peter Neuer, born on March 27, 1986, in Gelsenkirchen, West Germany, is a German goalkeeper who currently plays for FC Bayern Munich. Standing at 6 ft 4 in (193 cm), Neuer is widely regarded as one of the best goalkeepers in the world. Known for his exceptional shot-stopping ability, commanding presence, and distribution skills, he has redefined the role of a modern goalkeeper.

#26. Sven Ulreich

Sven Ulreich, born on August 3, 1988, in Schorndorf, Germany, is a German goalkeeper who has played for FC Bayern Munich. Standing at 192 cm, Ulreich has made significant contributions to the team during his time at the club. Ulreich joined FC Bayern Munich in 2015 and served as a reliable backup goalkeeper during his tenure. While he primarily played as the understudy to Manuel Neuer, Ulreich showcased his abilities whenever called upon. He demonstrated good shot-stopping skills, agility, and a strong presence between the posts.

#27. Yann Sommer

Yann Sommer was born on 17 December 1988 in Morges and plays for FC Bayern München. The 1.83m-tall goalkeeper was in the starting line-up in all previous 27 games of the current season. Of these, he won 14 games with his club. He was victorious over FC Schalke 04 while playing for FC Bayern München with a score of 6-0. He played as a goalie throughout this entire game.

#5. Benjamin Pavard

Benjamin Pavard, born on March 28, 1996, in Maubeuge, France, is a French international who joined Bayern Munich in 2019. Primarily deployed as a right-back or center-back, Pavard has made a significant impact on the team since his arrival. Known for his tactical intelligence, strong positional awareness, and physicality, he has been a key figure in the team's defensive line. He has provided flexibility to Bayern Munich's tactical setup. Whether playing as a full-back or a center-back, he displays composure on the ball, good passing ability, and the willingness to join the attack when required.

#19. Alphonso Davies

Alphonso Davies, born on November 2, 2000, in Buduburam, Ghana, is a Canadian left-back who has been playing for Bayern Munich since 2018. Davies' rise to prominence has been nothing short of remarkable, making him one of the most exciting young talents in world football. Davies is known for his ability to take on defenders, create chances, and contribute with goals and assists. During his time at Bayern Munich, Davies has showcased his versatility by excelling both as a left-back and as a left winger. His performances have earned him widespread recognition and accolades, including being named the 2020 UEFA Champions League Defender of the Season.

#20. Bouna Sarr

Bouna Sarr, born on January 31, 1992, in Lyon, France, is a French right-back who joined Bayern Munich in 2020. Sarr's signing from Olympique de Marseille demonstrated Bayern's intent to strengthen their defensive options. Sarr's attacking instincts have been evident during his appearances for Bayern Munich, as he contributes to the team's build-up play and offers an additional threat going forward. His work rate, defensive capabilities, and willingness to support the attack have made him a valuable asset for the team.

#21. Lucas Hernandez

Lucas Hernandez, born on February 14, 1996, in Marseille, France, is a French international who joined Bayern Munich in 2019. Midfielders

#6. Joshua Kimmich

Joshua Kimmich, born on February 8, 1995, in Rottweil, Germany, is a versatile footballer who plays primarily as a central midfielder but is also capable of playing as a right-back. Kimmich joined Bayern Munich in 2015 and has since established himself as one of the key figures in the team. Kimmich's impact on the pitch is undeniable. Known for his exceptional work rate, tactical intelligence, and technical skills, he possesses a wide range of attributes that make him a complete midfielder. He is equally adept at initiating attacks, providing key passes, and contributing defensively.

#8. Leon Goretzka

Leon Goretzka, born on February 6, 1995, in Bochum, Germany, is a dynamic midfielder who plays for Bayern Munich. Goretzka joined the club in 2018 and quickly made an impact with his performances on the pitch. Since joining Bayern Munich, Goretzka has showcased his versatility by adapting to different midfield roles. He has the vision to distribute accurate passes, the ability to win aerial duels, and the stamina to cover ground effectively. Goretzka's dynamic playing style and tireless work rate have made him a fan favorite.

#14. Paul Wanner

Paul Wanner (born 23 December 2005) is a professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Bundesliga club Bayern Munich. Born in Austria, he is a youth international for Germany. Wanner played for the youth team of FV Ravensburg, before joining Bayern Munich's youth team in mid-2018. Wanner was called up by Julian Nagelsmann to the Bayern Munich first team in January 2022, as many of the team's regulars were missing after testing positive for COVID-19.

#38. Ryan Gravenberch

Ryan Jiro Gravenberch (born 16 May 2002) is a Dutch professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Bundesliga club Bayern Munich and the Netherlands national team. Gravenberch is a progressive midfield player who looks to move the ball up the pitch whenever possible. Gravenberch is at his best as a ball-carrying, line-breaking No. 8, who is also capable of dropping deeper to build up play from the No. 6 position. He is far from the finished product, but he slots in well as a midfielder who carries possession before linking with the attacking players, while also serving as a goal threat.
